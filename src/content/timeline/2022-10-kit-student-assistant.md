The role combined research-oriented forecasting work with day-to-day academic support, which made it a particularly formative bridge between coursework and applied data science.

## Focus Areas
`Probabilistic time series forecasting`, `Infectious disease modelling`, `Research-oriented implementation in Python and R`, `Presentation and communication of technical results`


## Bachelor Thesis - DeepAR for Infectious Disease Forecasting

Achieved a bachelor's thesis grade of `1.0` by implementing a `DeepAR` pipeline to forecast influenza incidence across German regions. A key idea was to use the global nature of DeepAR to learn from repeated temporal patterns across multiple related regional time series instead of modelling each region in isolation.

I compared the model against a classical statistical `hhh4` baseline as well as a simple `feed-forward NN` benchmark. The probabilistic forecasts from DeepAR showed the strongest overall results and gave me a first deeper exposure to modern sequence modelling, uncertainty-aware forecasting, and proper benchmark-driven evaluation.
