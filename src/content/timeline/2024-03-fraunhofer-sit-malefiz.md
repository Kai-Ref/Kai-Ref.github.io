Parallel to my master's studies in Data Science in Mannheim, I worked as a Student Research Assistant at the Fraunhofer Institute for Secure Information Technology (SIT) within the MaLeFiz research project. The project focused on applying state-of-the-art machine learning and graph-based methods to detect financial crime, bridging academic research and practical investigative workflows.

The role provided hands-on experience in transferring recent scientific advances into applied solutions while collaborating with interdisciplinary research partners, including lawyers, social scientists and domain experts in financial crime investigation, who weren't familiar with AI methods. Thus, a particular focus was placed on making complex model outputs understandable and actionable for non-technical stakeholders.

## Focus Areas

`Financial crime detection`, `Graph-based machine learning`, `Explainable AI`, `Synthetic transaction data modeling`, `Research prototyping and visualization`, `Plotly Dashboards`

## Key Responsibilities

* Evaluated and adapted state-of-the-art machine learning and graph-based approaches for the detection of financial crime in collaboration with academic and industry research partners.
* Designed and generated synthetic financial transaction data to support experimentation, model development and validation activities.
* Developed a demonstrator application that visualized model outputs and investigation pathways, enabling transparent communication of results to non-technical stakeholders.
* Contributed to the translation of current research findings into practical tools for fraud and financial crime analysis via an interactive plotly dashboard.
