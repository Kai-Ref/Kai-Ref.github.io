Parallel to my bachelor's studies in Karlsruhe, I worked at ICIS for six months as a Student Data Scientist focused on EU power markets. The role gave me an early opportunity to apply data science in a commercially relevant forecasting setting, where model quality, interpretability, and operational usefulness mattered at the same time.

My work focused on short-term electricity market projects and exposed me to how predictive models are actually embedded into industry workflows rather than evaluated only in isolation.

## Focus Areas

`Short-term electricity price forecasting`, `Feature engineering and market signal classification`, `Production-oriented backtesting and evaluation`, `Translating model outputs into customer-facing analytics`

## Key Responsibilities

- Implemented an OTC backtesting statistic on the company website to evaluate forecasts more thoroughly. The statistic became part of the production flow and was also exposed to customers through the web interface.
- Developed and refined classification algorithms to identify price equalities and convergence patterns between European borders.
- Contributed to turning analytical findings into inputs for downstream forecasting models used in operational market analysis.

<div class='achievement'>
  📊 The classification work contributed to electricity price forecasts that were, on average, <strong>5%</strong> more accurate.
